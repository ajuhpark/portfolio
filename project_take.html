OVERVIEW

Background

My talented friends created an Instagram account called Secret Society of Artists where every week they would select a movie, game or tv show and everyone would draw and submit fan artwork for that property. Here are screenshots of the Instagram account:

Feed

Post

It looked great on the feed but there were a few things that were cumbersome about the process.

  1. People had to send in artwork to the account holder and the account holder had to upload it onto the page. There was little control on the formatting of the image and caption.
  2. The artist had to be tagged in the post in order for other users to see more of their work. The artist would not get notifications if there were comments or feedback on their post.

I thought there could be a better way to share artwork so I began by looking at the needs of the fan art community to define the problem.

Problem

The emergence of media and storytelling has given rise to the number of artists drawing fan art. Artists give their creative interpretations on different shows, movies and books that expand and personalize the storytelling experience.

Although the community of fan artists is large, there isn't a singular online hub for fan artwork. There is a gap in the market for an app that allows users to save, organize and upload fan artwork.

Solution

‘Take’ is an app focused specifically on fan art. It has an image based feed and can be filtered and categorized by the different shows and movies as well as the different mediums of artwork.

I began the project with a wider scope of looking to solve issues that users have with cloud services. I took a user survey to see how participants use cloud services and found that they often have the same goals.

5
TESTING

I moderated user-testing for different tasks on my wireframes and it mad me look at different ways to organize content on my page. Here are a few examples of changes I made.

Menu Bar

I wanted to give the users a place to start so I made a menu bar that would be categorized by movies, tv shows and other media. When a user would click on ‘movies’, they would see suggestions on different movies, popular posts and featured posts chosen by the staff.

Instead of organizing information in a menu bar, I decided to organize content in the feed by splitting the feed into different categories. I put in sections for recommended, trending posts and staff picks within the user’s feed.

Filter

Based on user feedback, the content in the feed needed more structure and organization. I put in an expandable filter section where users could select which media they wanted to see in the feed along with an option for timing to see posts from different time frames (today, this week, this month...). Inside the filter, I also included a section for popular tags to give users a starting ground.

Banner

When I was user testing, a few participants were unsure of what the site was for. Because it involved different types of media, some people mistook it for a streaming service like Hulu or Netflix.

To make the purpose of the site more clear, I placed a banner at the top of the website with the site mission statement stating that it’s a website for creative fan content.

Conclusion

One of my main takeaways from this project was to make things obvious and clear from the start.

After performing user testing on my first mockup, I was surprised to see that there was confusion on the function and purpose of the app. The mixture of media and art for the content threw some people off. Because the idea of 'Take' had been brewing in my mind, I mistakenly assumed that my users would be on the same wavelength and be off and running once they clicked into it.

After seeing which parts of the app were difficult to initially grasp, I tried to make everything as obvious and clear as possible. I put a large banner with the 'Take' mission statement on the top of the website. I put the home, search and account buttons where other social media apps would normally have them. I tried to adapt features from Youtube and VSCO for the feed so the organization of the content would look familiar but the layout would still look fresh and specific to 'Take'.

'Take' was created with the foundation of user research, surveying and testing. Through different iterations, this app hopefully accomplished the goal of creating a collaborative service where users could save, organize and upload fan artwork.
