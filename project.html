Overview

Background

My talented friends created an Instagram account called Secret Society of Artists where every week they would select a movie, game or tv show and everyone would draw and submit fan artwork for that property. Here are screenshots of the Instagram account:

Feed

Post

It looked great on the feed but there were a few things that were cumbersome about the process.

  1. People had to send in artwork to the account holder and the account holder had to upload it onto the page. There was little control on the formatting of the image and caption.
  2. The artist had to be tagged in the post in order for other users to see more of their work. The artist would not get notifications if there were comments or feedback on their post.

I thought there could be a better way to share artwork so I began by looking at the needs of the fan art community to define the problem.

Problem

The emergence of media and storytelling has given rise to the number of artists drawing fan art. Fan art propagates movies, tv shows and comics and there is a large community of artists that give their interpretations on different shows and properties.

Although there is a large community, there isn't a singular online hub for fan artwork. There is a gap in the market for an app that allows users to organize, share and filter through fan artwork.

Solution

‘Take’ is an app focused specifically on fan content. It has an image based feed and can be filtered and categorized by the different shows and movies as well as the different mediums of artwork.
